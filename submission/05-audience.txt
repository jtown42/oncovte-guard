Primary users are clinicians who manage ambulatory cancer patients: medical oncologists, hematologist-oncologists, oncology pharmacists, and advanced practice providers (NPs/PAs). The CDS Hooks service additionally targets EHR and informatics teams who want to embed guideline-concordant VTE prophylaxis prompts into existing order-entry and chart-review workflows without adopting a separate application. The ultimate beneficiary is the ambulatory cancer patient, for whom appropriate, interaction-aware thromboprophylaxis reduces both preventable VTE and avoidable anticoagulant-related bleeding.
